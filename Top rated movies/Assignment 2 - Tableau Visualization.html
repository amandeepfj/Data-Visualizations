    ISOM 675 Data Visualization

    Homework Assignment 2 - Tableau Visualization

    Description

    Your task is to design a static (i.e., single image) visualization that you believe effectively communicates one of the two attached data sets. You can choose either data set to create your visualization. No other data (e.g., from external sources) should be used. You will also provide a short write-up (no more than 4 paragraphs) describing your design. While you must use one of the data sets given, note that you are free to filter, transform and augment the data as you see fit to highlight the elements that you think are most important in the data set. You must use Tableau to create your visualization, but you can augment/transform/filter your data outside of Tableau if you wish.  

    As different visualizations can emphasize different aspects of a data set, you should document what aspects of the data you are attempting to most effectively communicate. In short, what is your quantitative message? Just as important, also note which aspects of the data might be obscured or down-played due to your visualization design. You do not have to visualize every dimension of the data.

    In your write-up, you should provide a rigorous rationale for your design decisions. Document the visual encodings you used and why they are appropriate for the data. These decisions include the choice of visualization type, size, color, scale, and other visual elements, as well as the use of sorting or other data transformations. How do these decisions facilitate effective communication? Also, describe your use of ggplot2 and any additional R packages in the production of your design. In other words, how did you make it?

    Dataset Options

    1. recent-grads.csv contains data on the earnings of college majors from American Community Survey 2010-2012 Public Use Microdata Series.

      Required Deliverables (files to upload to Canvas Assignment)

      1. Visualization a single PDF file that contains
        1. Your static visualization. Make sure to size your image so it will fit within a reasonable window size on a laptop screen and the text is readable at that size.
        2. Your short write-up as outlined above.
      2. A Tableau packaged workbook (.twbx) file.

      Submission Details

      This is an individual assignment. You may not work in groups. Your completed assignment is due on Sunday October 21st, at 11:59 pm. We will be discussing submissions in class the week of the 23rd, so be sure to avoid a late submission!  Any HW submitted after the deadline will be penalized 50% of the possible points if it is submitted within 24 hours of the deadline. No submissions more than 24 hours past the deadline will be accepted.

      To submit your assignment, click on Assignment 1 in Canvas and follow the instructions. You can upload multiple files by clicking the Add Another File button.

      Grading

      Assignment grades will be based on the following evaluation criteria

      • The interestingness of the story or quantitative message you are trying to tell
      • How well your visualization design communicates your story/quantitative message
      • The technical execution of your visualization
      • Your explanations and justifications of your design choices
      • The overall aesthetic and effectiveness of your design

      Peer Review

      On October 23rd, you will be assigned 5-10 visualizations from other students to peer review. Please complete these in a timely manner and follow the instructions in the rubric. Your peer reviews are anonymous.
